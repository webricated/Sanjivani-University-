Behavioural Interview & STAR Technique

TY Corporate Readiness – Semester VI

1. Behavioural Interview – Introduction

A Behavioural Interview is a structured interview technique used by recruiters to evaluate a candidate’s past behaviour in real-life situations to predict future performance.

Key Principle:
Past behaviour is the best indicator of future performance.

2. Purpose of Behavioural Interviews

Behavioural interviews help employers assess the following skills:

  • Problem-solving ability
  • Communication skills
  • Teamwork and leadership
  • Decision-making under pressure
  • Conflict management
  • Time management
  • Adaptability and integrity

3. Common Behavioural Interview Questions

  • Tell me about a time you faced a challenge.
  • Describe a situation where you handled conflict in a team.
  • Give an example of a failure and what you learned.
  • Tell me about a time you worked under pressure.
  • Describe a situation where you showed leadership.

4. What Interviewers Look For

  • Clarity of thought
  • Structured answers
  • Ownership of actions
  • Learning mindset
  • Professional attitude

5. STAR Technique – Overview

The STAR Technique is a structured method used to answer behavioural interview questions clearly and effectively.

S – Situation
T – Task
A – Action
R – Result

6. STAR Technique – Detailed Explanation

S – Situation

Describe the background and context of the situation.

  • Where did it happen?
  • What was the challenge?
Example:
During my final year project, our team was facing delays due to poor coordination.

T – Task

Explain your responsibility or role in that situation.

  • What was expected from you?
  • What goal did you have?
Example:
I was responsible for coordinating tasks and ensuring timely submission.

A – Action

Describe the actions you personally took to solve the problem.

  • Focus on your contribution
  • Mention skills, tools, or decisions used
Example:
I created a task timeline, assigned responsibilities, and conducted daily follow-ups.

R – Result

Explain the outcome of your actions.

  • Mention measurable results if possible
  • Highlight success or learning
Example:
As a result, the project was completed on time and received an excellent evaluation.

7. Sample STAR Answer

Question: Tell me about a time you solved a problem.

Situation: System errors were delaying reports during my internship.
Task: I was asked to identify and reduce the errors.
Action: I analyzed logs and automated validation checks.
Result: Error rate reduced by 40%, improving efficiency.

8. Do’s and Don’ts of STAR Technique

Do’s

  • Be honest and specific
  • Focus on your role
  • Highlight learning outcomes

Don’ts

  • Avoid exaggeration
  • Do not blame others
  • Do not give vague answers
  • Never skip the result

9. Classroom Practice Activity

Answer the following using the STAR technique:

  • A time you failed and recovered
  • A leadership experience
  • A conflict with a teammate

10. Summary

  • Behavioural interviews focus on real past experiences
  • STAR technique ensures structured and clear answers
  • Effective use improves interview success significantly
